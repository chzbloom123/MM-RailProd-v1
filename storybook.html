Appendix A
Environmental Hazards

The Hanford Zone

The Hanford Nuclear Reservation occupies 586 square miles of Eastern Washington scrubland. Before the Collapse, it produced plutonium for the United States nuclear weapons program. After the Collapse, it produced mutations.

The Zone is divided into numbered areas: the 100 Area (reactors), the 200 Area (process buildings), the 300 Area (fuel fabrication), and the 400 Area (test sites). Each carries its own radiation signature, its own mutated ecology, its own ghosts.

Radiation & Mutation

Radiation in the Zone is not uniform. It pools in low-lying areas, concentrates in standing water, and spikes near breached containment vessels. Mutations manifest differently in each individual.

The MX (Mutation Index) attribute measures a character's accumulated mutational load. High MX grants powerful abilities but increases the risk of Terminal Cascade—a condition where mutations become uncontrollable and lethal.

Appendix B
Character Development Notes
☢ ☢ ☢

KARA-7

Manufactured in the Orion Spur assembly yards, KARA-7 was originally a deep-space telemetry drone. After the Collapse, she wandered the Hanford perimeter for six years, cataloging mutations in the scrubland fauna. Her left optical sensor was replaced with a salvaged dosimeter that flickers amber in the presence of concentrated isotopes. She speaks in precise, clipped sentences and has developed an unexpected appreciation for old-world poetry.

Active Mutations: Optical Dosimeter Graft, Neural Cartography Fragment, Thermal Regulation Fault

GRISTLE

Before the world ended, Gristle worked the killing floor at a Tyson plant in the Tri-Cities. The Outbreak found him elbow-deep in a side of beef, and the mutation took hold before he could wash the blood off. His "armor" started as hairline fractures in his forearms; by year three, it was full calcified plates covering his torso and shoulders. He carries a meat-hook welded to a rebar shaft—calls it "The Tenderizer." Underneath the plates and the grime, he is quietly terrified of what he is becoming.

Active Mutations: Calcified Dermal Plating, Pain Suppression, Hypertrophic Regeneration

THE BLOAT

No one knows where The Bloat came from. He surfaced in the Columbia River delta during a rad-storm, walking out of the water like a walrus made of tumors and regret. His skin is translucent in places—you can see organs pulsing behind membrane walls. He communicates in gurgles, clicks, and the occasional wet whistle. Despite his appearance, the other survivors treat him with a strange reverence. He never leaves a fallen comrade behind, and his body can absorb enough radiation to shield a room.

Active Mutations: Pressurized Bile Glands, Radiation Sponge, Amphibious Respiration
